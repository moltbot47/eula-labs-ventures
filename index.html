The Vision

EULA Labs — Engineered Units of Leverage & Automation — is building an AI research, education, and compute center in Houston, TX. A physical campus where open-source robotics, community-governed AI agents, and developer training operate under one roof.

We believe AI agents will become as common and essential as social media platforms are today. The question is whether that future is owned by a few corporations or governed by the communities that build it.

Our model is simple: open-source research produces public goods — robotics designs, agent frameworks, educational content. Managed services built on those foundations generate revenue. Revenue flows back to contributors through a transparent, contribution-weighted system.

The Pembrook compound provides hard asset backing for every dollar invested. The research produces real products. The infrastructure scales through community participation.

Regulation Crowdfunding (Reg CF)

SEC-regulated community fundraising. Anyone can invest — accredited or not. Transparent, democratic, and aligned with our mission.

What Is Reg CF?

Regulation Crowdfunding allows companies to raise up to $5M per year from the general public through SEC-registered funding portals like Wefunder, Republic, or StartEngine. Investors browse your campaign, commit funds (held in escrow), and receive securities when the target is met.

This is the most community-aligned fundraising path — it democratizes access to AI infrastructure investment the same way we democratize access to AI agents.

How It Works

  1. Prepare & File Form CSecurities attorney prepares offering documents. File Form C with the SEC. 2 weeks.
  2. Launch on Funding PortalCampaign goes live on Wefunder, Republic, or StartEngine. Runs 2–3 months.
  3. Investors Commit FundsAnyone can invest. Funds held in escrow until target is reached.
  4. Target Met → Funds ReleasedEscrow releases funds at $500K minimum target, minus platform fees (6–8%). Campaign stays open up to $2M maximum. Capital deploys in milestones as raised.
  5. Issue Membership InterestsInvestors receive LLC membership units — legal, SEC-compliant securities.
  6. Annual ReportingFile Form C-AR annually. Transparent reporting to all investors.
